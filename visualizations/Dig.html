Operator Profile

Digi Communications

Leading Pay-TV and Fixed Internet Provider in Romania

Company & Structure

Background: Digi Communications is headquartered in the Netherlands (statutory seat) but the effective management and its major business is in Romania. The Romanian entity (formerly RCS & RDS) traces back to early 1990s cable beginnings and now operates under the "Digi" brand (cable, broadband, mobile, TV) through Digi Romania S.A.

Key People: Founder/majority shareholder Zoltán Teszári; CEO of the group: Serghei Bulgac.

Scope & Financials

  • Group revenues: €1.70 billion in 2023
  • Romania revenue: ~€1.13 billion (2023)
  • Major credit facilities secured in March 2025

Market Position

  • ~6.6 million cable subscribers (2022)
  • ~69% cable TV market share
  • Leading pay-TV and fixed internet provider in Romania

Services & Business Segments

  • Cable & Satellite TV: Historically strong via the Digi TV brand
  • Broadband Internet: Comprehensive fibre/hybrid network (FTTB/FTTH)
  • Mobile: Digi Mobil operator, 5G launched in 2019
  • Business Services: DIGI Internet Business (150 Mbps - 1 Gbps)
  • Media Channels: Own TV channels (Digi Sport, Digi 24)

Competitive & Strategic Strengths

  • High penetration/fixed-line dominance with scale and distribution
  • Own infrastructure & fibre rollout (FTTB → FTTH)
  • Bundle offerings (triple/quad-play packages)
  • Cost-focused strategy with low OPEX
  • Growth potential via mobile & acquisitions

Risks & Challenges

  • Mobile growth and competitive pressure
  • Regulatory/spectrum/rights costs
  • Margin pressure from competitive pricing
  • Debt/finance risk from large investments
  • Technology & substitution risk (OTT/streaming)
  • Consolidation/regulatory approval challenges

Strengths

  • Market leading position in fixed broadband/cable TV
  • Strong infrastructure (fibre, cable) and scale
  • Ability to bundle services (TV + broadband + mobile)
  • Cost-focused strategy, relatively low OPEX
  • Momentum for growth (via mobile, regional expansion)

Weaknesses

  • Mobile business less dominant and margin likely lower
  • Heavy CAPEX/investment needed for network upgrades
  • Dependence on content rights/licensing for TV business

Opportunities

  • Consolidation via M&A (e.g., acquisition of mobile assets)
  • Growth in broadband/FTTH as households upgrade
  • Exploit bundling and cross-selling in converged services
  • Monetise streaming/OTT or next-gen entertainment services
  • Regional expansion or replication of Romanian model

Threats

  • Intense competition (fixed, mobile, OTT) eroding prices/margins
  • Regulatory changes (spectrum, broadcast rights, net neutrality)
  • Technology shift away from traditional cable/satellite TV
  • Debt/financial risk if investments don't pay off
  • Potential disruption from new entrants, OTT players

Recent Strategic Moves & Outlook

  • Acquisition Activity: In September 2025, Digi Romania and Vodafone Romania agreed to acquire separate parts of Telekom Romania Mobile Communications
  • Financing: In March 2025 the group secured major credit facilities to support expansion across Romania, Portugal, Spain
  • Network Upgrades: Already live with 5G in Romania (2019)
  • Strategy: Emphasis on high-quality telecom services at competitive prices, network optimisation and low cost infrastructure

Business Summary

For Romania, Digi Communications appears very strong in its core: fixed broadband + cable TV. With ~6.6 million cable subscribers and ~69% market share, they have scale, distribution, and leverage. The challenge is in the mobile side (where they are less dominant) and in managing transitions (network upgrades + competition + technology shifts).

Assessment: The "core" (fixed + TV) is robust with defensive characteristics, but growth and margin improvement will likely come from mobile, bundles, and new offerings — which carry more risk. The business is well-positioned but needs execution.
