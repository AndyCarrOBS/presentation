- **Household count**: ~4.8 million households
- **Average household size**: ~2.2 people

### Age Structure
- **0-14**: ~17%
- **15-24**: ~12%
- **25-44**: ~26%
- **45-64**: ~27%
- **65+**: ~18%
- **Median age**: ~41 years

### Household Types

- **Family households with children**: ~25%

- **Socioeconomic segmentation**: ABC1 ~55%, C2DE ~45%

## 5. Language, Culture & Content Consumption

### Language
- **Primary language**: Swedish
- **% bilingual population**: ~85% (English as second language)
- **Requirements**: Subtitling preferred over dubbing

### Content Preferences
- **Top genres**: News, sports, movies, drama series, crime dramas, documentaries
- **Local content importance**: High - Swedish content preferred
- **Sports rights**: Football (Allsvenskan), Ice Hockey, Skiing
- **Public service broadcaster influence**: Very strong (SVT)

## 6. Regulatory Environment

### Broadcast Regulation
- **Licensing authorities**: Swedish Media Authority
- **Spectrum allocation**: DTT (MUX1-5), satellite (SES, Eutelsat)
- **Must-carry rules**: Yes - public channels must be carried
- **Local content quota**: European content quotas apply

### Consumer & Platform Regulation
- **Anti-piracy environment**: Strong enforcement
- **CAS/DRM compliance**: Mandatory for pay-TV
- **CI+ certification requirements**: Yes, via CI+ Consortium
- **Taxes on TV subscriptions**: VAT 25%, TV licence fee (abolished 2019)
- **Data protection**: GDPR compliant

## 9. Competitive Landscape

### Major Operators

**Tele2**
- Platform type: Cable, IPTV, OTT
- Subscriber numbers: ~2.17 million (Q3 2025 - mobile/TV combined)

- Technology: CI+, operator STBs, OTT apps

- Subscriber numbers: ~500,000

- Subscriber numbers: ~1-1.5 million
