# OTT Platform Analysis: Germany & Neighboring Countries
## Local OTT Players, HbbTV vs Proprietary Apps, TV Support & Marketing Partnerships

---

## Executive Summary

This document provides a comprehensive analysis of local OTT players across Germany and its nine neighboring countries, detailing application types (HbbTV vs proprietary), TV manufacturer whitelisting/testing requirements, current TV support, and joint marketing partnerships.

**Key Findings:**
- **HbbTV Dominance**: Public service broadcasters (ARD, ZDF, ORF, SRG SSR) heavily use HbbTV applications triggered from broadcast
- **Proprietary Apps**: Commercial broadcasters (RTL+, Joyn, TF1+, etc.) primarily use proprietary applications requiring porting
- **Regulatory Impact**: Belgium mandates pre-installed apps (VRT Max), influencing TV manufacturer strategies
- **TV Support**: Samsung Tizen and LG webOS have extensive support, Android TV growing, platform-specific requirements vary

## 11. TV Manufacturer Requirements Summary

### 11.1 Samsung Tizen

**Whitelisting/Testing Requirements:**
- **Application Packaging**: `.wgt` files for web applications, `.tpk` files for .NET applications
- **Privileges**: Three levels (public, partner, platform) - partner-level APIs require Samsung Content Manager authorization
- **Compliance**: Must comply with Tizen Compliance Specification
- **WebView**: Based on Chromium version 47 or higher
- **Certification**: Required before distribution
- **Pre-test Phase**: Unauthorized use of privileged APIs results in application blocking

**Support Status:**
- ✅ Most local OTT players supported
- ✅ HbbTV support varies by broadcaster
- ✅ Proprietary apps require certification

### 11.2 LG webOS

**Whitelisting/Testing Requirements:**
- **App Self-Checklist**: Required before submission (content, UX, privacy, credential information)
- **QA Process**: Prerequisite for app distribution
- **Security**: Digital signature verification, sandboxed execution, whitelisted directories
- **Whitelist Rule Creation**: Developers can create Whitelist Rule files for native apps
- **Certification**: Required before distribution

**Support Status:**
- ✅ Most local OTT players supported
- ✅ HbbTV 2.0 support (M6+ confirmed)
- ✅ Proprietary apps require certification

### 11.3 Android TV

**Whitelisting/Testing Requirements:**
- **Security Testing**: Comprehensive security testing required (PTaaS available)
- **Compatibility Testing**: Testing across different Android TV devices and versions
- **Screen Sizes**: Testing for different screen sizes and resolutions
- **Hardware Capabilities**: Testing for different hardware capabilities
- **Open Platform**: More open than Tizen/webOS, but security testing still required

**Support Status:**
- ✅ Most local OTT players supported
- ✅ HbbTV integration possible (Joyn confirmed on Sony/Philips)
- ✅ Proprietary apps require testing

## 12. HbbTV vs Proprietary Applications: Key Insights

### 12.1 HbbTV Applications (Broadcast-Triggered)

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Standardized approach (HbbTV standard)
- ✅ Triggered from broadcast signal (Red Button)
- ✅ No app store approval required
- ✅ Works across compatible TVs automatically
- ✅ Lower development/maintenance costs

**Disadvantages:**
- ⚠️ Requires HbbTV-compatible TV
- ⚠️ Limited to broadcast-triggered activation
- ⚠️ Less flexibility than native apps
- ⚠️ Dependent on broadcast signal

**Typical Users:**
- Public service broadcasters (ARD, ZDF, ORF, SRG SSR, ČT, TVP, DR)
- Some commercial broadcasters (M6+)

### 12.2 Proprietary Applications (Requires Porting)

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Full control over user experience
- ✅ Can be accessed independently of broadcast
- ✅ More features and customization
- ✅ App store presence and discovery

**Disadvantages:**
- ⚠️ Requires porting to each platform (Tizen, webOS, Android TV, etc.)
- ⚠️ App store approval process
- ⚠️ Platform-specific testing and certification
- ⚠️ Higher development/maintenance costs
- ⚠️ Whitelisting/testing requirements vary by platform

**Typical Users:**
- Commercial broadcasters (RTL+, Joyn, TF1+, TV2 Play, Player.pl, Polsat Box Go, Oneplay)
- Some public service broadcasters (also offer proprietary apps alongside HbbTV)

## 14. Recommendations for Smart TV Platform Entry

### 14.1 Application Strategy

**For Public Service Broadcaster Partnerships:**
- **HbbTV Support**: Essential - Public service broadcasters heavily use HbbTV
- **Proprietary Apps**: Also important - Most offer both HbbTV and proprietary apps
- **Red Button Functionality**: Must support HbbTV Red Button for broadcast-triggered activation

**For Commercial Broadcaster Partnerships:**
- **Proprietary Apps**: Primary focus - Commercial broadcasters primarily use proprietary apps
- **Platform Porting**: Budget for porting to Tizen, webOS, Android TV, Fire TV, Apple TV
- **Certification**: Factor in certification costs and timelines

### 14.2 TV Manufacturer Engagement

**Priority Platforms:**
1. **Samsung Tizen** - Market leader, extensive support
2. **LG webOS** - Significant market share, HbbTV 2.0 support
3. **Android TV** - Growing, HbbTV integration possible (Joyn example)

**Certification Strategy:**
- Start with Samsung Tizen and LG webOS (highest market share)
- Then expand to Android TV (growing market)
- Consider Fire TV and Apple TV (premium segments)

### 14.3 Regulatory Considerations

**Belgium:**
- VRT Max pre-installation mandate - Factor into Belgium market entry strategy
- May require pre-installation agreements with TV manufacturers

**Germany:**
- ARD/ZDF position paper on HbbTV requirements - Ensure compliance
- Respect content rights (Joyn legal issue example)

### 14.4 Partnership Opportunities

**Technical Partnerships:**
- HbbTV integration partnerships (Verance example)
- Addressable TV partnerships (ATVI example)
- App development partnerships (DOTSCREEN example)

**Marketing Partnerships:**
- Limited joint marketing opportunities identified
- Focus on technical integration and regulatory compliance
