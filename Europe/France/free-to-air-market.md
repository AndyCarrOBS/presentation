# France Free-to-Air TV Market Analysis

## Executive Summary

France's free-to-air TV market is dominated by DTT (44.7% of households), with satellite (Fransat) and cable also significant. The market is characterized by strong public service broadcasters (France Télévisions), major commercial broadcasters (TF1, M6 Group), and a growing ecosystem of local OTT services that hero French content.

---

## 1. Free-to-Air Platform Distribution

### 1.1 Platform Breakdown (2024-2025)

**Total TV Households: ~26.5 million**

#### • Digital Terrestrial Television (DTT / TNT)
- **Households**: 44.7% of French households (December 2023)
- **Year-on-year change**: -2% (2023)
- **Primary delivery method**: DVB-T2 HD terrestrial network
- **Coverage**: Nationwide
- **Key characteristics**:
  - Free-to-air HD channels
  - UHD rollout: France 2 in UHD via DTT (16M inhabitants January 2024, 35M+ June 2024, 52% of population)
  - No subscription required
  - Most popular delivery method
  - New channel numbering system implemented June 6, 2025
  - New channels: T18 (channel 18), NOVO19 (channel 19, effective September 1, 2025)

#### • Satellite Reception (Fransat)
- **Households**: ~2 million households equipped (June 2025)
- **Primary delivery method**: Eutelsat satellite system
- **Coverage**: Nationwide (for areas with limited/no terrestrial reception)
- **Key characteristics**: 
  - Free-to-air DTT channels via satellite
  - France 2 UHD available via Fransat (immediate nationwide coverage)
  - Operated by Eutelsat
  - Serves areas with limited terrestrial access

#### • Cable Reception (Free-to-Air Tier)
- **Households**: Data not publicly disclosed separately
- **Primary delivery method**: DOCSIS cable networks
- **Coverage**: Urban and suburban areas
- **Key characteristics**:
  - Basic cable tier includes free-to-air channels
  - Must-carry rules ensure France Télévisions availability
  - Declining traditional cable subscriptions

#### • Internet-Based Reception (IPTV/OTT)
- **Households**: Growing trend
- **Primary delivery method**: IPTV, streaming apps, smart TV apps
- **Key characteristics**:
  - IPTV services (Orange, Free, SFR, Bouygues Telecom)
  - Streaming apps
  - Hybrid consumption (linear + streaming)

### 1.2 Platform Trends

**Key Developments:**
- **DTT**: Most popular (44.7% of households) but declining (-2%)
- **UHD Rollout**: France 2 UHD via DTT (52% of population by June 2024, targeting 70% by July 2024)
- **Fransat**: ~2 million households equipped
- **Channel Changes**: New channel numbering (June 2025), new channels (T18, NOVO19)
- **Canal+ Withdrawal**: Canal+ withdrawing 4 pay-TV channels from DTT (effective June 2025)
- **NRJ 12 Closure**: Ceased broadcasting March 1, 2025

## 2. Main Free-to-Air Broadcasters

### 2.1 Public Service Broadcasters

#### • **France Télévisions**
- **Market Share**: ~29.4% of French TV broadcasting sector (2024)
- **Position**: Leading public service broadcaster
- **Structure**: National public broadcaster
- **Main Channels**:
  - **France 2** - Main national channel (UHD available via DTT and Fransat)
  - **France 3** - Regional and information
  - **France 4** - Entertainment (assigned to channel 4, June 2025)
  - **France 5** - Culture and documentaries
  - **France Info** - News channel
- **Reach**: Near universal coverage (public service broadcaster)
- **Content Focus**: News, entertainment, documentaries, regional content, cultural content
- **Funding**: License fee + advertising (limited)
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Leading public service broadcaster (~29.4% market share)
  - Strong news and information programming
  - Regional content production
  - High-quality documentaries
  - Cultural content
  - UHD leadership (France 2 UHD via DTT and Fransat)
  - Must-carry on all platforms

### 2.2 Commercial Broadcasters

#### • **TF1 Group**
- **Market Share**: 18.7% total audience share (2024)
- **Position**: #1 commercial broadcaster
- **Main Channels**:
  - **TF1** - Main commercial channel
  - **TMC** - Entertainment
  - **TFX** - Entertainment
  - **TF1 Séries Films** - Series and movies
  - **LCI** - News channel
- **Reach**: Leading commercial broadcaster
- **Content Focus**: Entertainment, series, news, movies
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Market leader (18.7% total audience share)
  - Strong entertainment programming
  - Local content production
  - Series and movie acquisitions
- **OTT Service**: **TF1+** - Leading free streaming platform (78% assisted awareness, 2024)

#### • **M6 Group**
- **Market Share**: 7.8% total audience share (2024)
- **Position**: Major commercial broadcaster
- **Main Channels**:
  - **M6** - Main commercial channel
  - **W9** - Entertainment
  - **6ter** - Entertainment
  - **Gulli** - Kids channel (relocated to channel 12, June 2025)
- **Reach**: Major commercial broadcaster
- **Content Focus**: Entertainment, series, kids content
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Strong entertainment programming
  - Local content production
  - Kids programming (Gulli)
- **OTT Service**: **M6+** - Strong digital presence (Euro 2024 performance)

#### • **Arte**
- **Market Share**: Cultural channel
- **Position**: Franco-German cultural channel
- **Reach**: Available across France and Germany
- **Content Focus**: Cultural content, documentaries
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Franco-German cultural channel
  - High-quality documentaries
  - Cultural programming

## 3. Local OTT Services (Heroing French Content)

### 3.1 Commercial Broadcaster OTT Services

#### • **TF1+**

- **Subscribers**: Free streaming platform

- **Content Focus**:

  - **Live streaming**: Live streams of TF1 Group channels

  - Free streaming platform

  - French content focus
  - Leading free streaming platform (78% awareness)
- **Platforms**: Web, mobile apps, smart TV apps, streaming devices
- **Market Position**: Leading free streaming platform for francophones

#### • **M6+**
- **Owner**: M6 Group
- **Subscribers**: Data not publicly disclosed
- **Content Focus**:
  - **Entertainment**: M6 Group content, series
  - **Kids**: Gulli content
  - **Catch-up**: Catch-up for M6 Group channels
  - **Live streaming**: Live streams of M6 Group channels
- **Key Features**:
  - Freemium model (free tier + premium tier)
  - M6 Group content library
  - French content focus
  - Strong digital presence (Euro 2024 performance)
- **Platforms**: Web, mobile apps, smart TV apps, streaming devices
- **Market Position**: Major commercial French OTT service

### 3.2 Public Service Broadcaster OTT Services

#### • **France Télévisions Streaming**
- **Owner**: France Télévisions (public service broadcaster)
- **Subscribers**: Free service (funded by license fee)

  - **Information**: News and information programming

  - Free access (no subscription)

## 5. Platform Technology & Delivery

### 5.1 Digital Terrestrial (DVB-T2 HD)
- **Free-to-Air Channels**: 20+ channels
- **HD Channels**: All channels in HD
- **UHD Channels**: France 2 UHD (52% of population by June 2024, targeting 70%)
- **Coverage**: 98%+ population coverage
- **Technology**: DVB-T2, HEVC, MPEG-4
- **Channel Numbering**: New system implemented June 6, 2025

### 5.2 Satellite (Fransat)
- **Free-to-Air Channels**: DTT channels via satellite
- **HD Channels**: Available in HD
- **UHD Channels**: France 2 UHD (immediate nationwide coverage)
- **Coverage**: Nationwide (for areas with limited/no terrestrial reception)
- **Technology**: DVB-S2, MPEG-4, HEVC
- **Households**: ~2 million equipped (June 2025)

### 5.3 Cable (DOCSIS)
- **Free-to-Air Tier**: Basic cable tier includes free-to-air channels
- **Must-Carry**: France Télévisions must be carried
- **HD Channels**: Available in HD
- **Coverage**: Urban and suburban areas
- **Technology**: DOCSIS 3.0/3.1, MPEG-4

### 5.4 Internet-Based (IPTV/OTT)
- **Catch-up Services**: TF1+, M6+, France Télévisions streaming
- **Live Streaming**: Live streams of free-to-air channels
- **Smart TV Apps**: Available on smart TVs
- **Streaming Devices**: Available on streaming media players
- **Technology**: HLS, DASH, DRM (for premium content)
