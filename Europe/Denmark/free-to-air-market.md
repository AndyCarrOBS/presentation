# Denmark Free-to-Air TV Market Analysis

## Executive Summary

Denmark's free-to-air TV market is experiencing significant transformation, with traditional TV viewing declining and streaming services growing rapidly. The market is characterized by strong public service broadcasters (DR, TV 2), major commercial broadcasters (TV3, Kanal 5), and a growing ecosystem of local OTT services that hero Danish content.

---

## 1. Free-to-Air Platform Distribution

### 1.1 Platform Breakdown (2025)

**Total TV Households: Data not publicly disclosed**

#### • Satellite Reception
- **Households**: Data not publicly disclosed separately
- **Primary delivery method**: Astra satellite
- **Coverage**: Near universal
- **Key characteristics**: 
  - Free-to-air channels available without subscription
  - HD channels available
  - Popular delivery method

#### • Cable Reception (Free-to-Air Tier)
- **Households**: Data not publicly disclosed separately
- **Primary delivery method**: DOCSIS cable networks (YouSee, Norlys, Stofa)
- **Coverage**: Urban and suburban areas
- **Key characteristics**:
  - Basic cable tier includes free-to-air channels
  - Must-carry rules ensure DR/TV 2 availability
  - Cable penetration high in Denmark

#### • Digital Terrestrial Television (DTT)
- **Households**: Data not publicly disclosed separately
- **Primary delivery method**: DVB-T2 terrestrial network
- **Coverage**: Nationwide
- **Key characteristics**:
  - Free-to-air HD channels
  - No subscription required
  - Cost-effective alternative

#### • Internet-Based Reception (IPTV/OTT)
- **Households**: Growing trend
- **Primary delivery method**: IPTV, streaming apps, smart TV apps
- **Key characteristics**:
  - 65% of video consumption via streaming/social video (2025)
  - Traditional TV viewing declined by 10+ minutes daily
  - Streaming services account for significant share

### 1.2 Platform Trends

**Key Developments:**
- **Traditional TV Decline**: Traditional TV viewing time decreased by over 10 minutes daily
- **Streaming Growth**: Streaming services and social video account for 65% of video consumption
- **Pay-TV Decline**: Traditional pay-TV subscriptions declining, ARPU below $31 (lowest since 2014)
- **OTT Growth**: Over 5 million paid streaming subscriptions, 500,000+ new subscriptions added

## 3. Local OTT Services (Heroing Danish Content)

### 3.1 Public Service Broadcaster OTT Services

#### • **DR TV**
- **Owner**: DR (public service broadcaster)
- **Subscribers**: Free service (funded by license fee)
- **Daily Usage**: 17 minutes per day (2025)
- **Content Focus**:
  - **Information**: News and information programming
  - **Entertainment**: DR series, documentaries, cultural content
  - **Catch-up**: 7-day catch-up for DR channels
  - **Live streaming**: Live streams of DR channels
  - **Kids Content**: DR Ramasjang, DR Ultra content
- **Key Features**:
  - Free access (no subscription)
  - Extensive catch-up library
  - High-quality documentaries and news
  - Danish content focus
  - Kids content
- **Platforms**: Web, mobile apps, smart TV apps, streaming devices
- **Market Position**: Leading public service OTT service

#### • **TV2 Play**
- **Owner**: TV 2 (public service broadcaster, commercial model)
- **Subscribers**: Matches Netflix subscriber numbers (Denmark)
- **Daily Usage**: 20 minutes per day (2025)
- **Content Focus**:
  - **Entertainment**: TV 2 series, entertainment, sports
  - **Sports**: TV 2 Sport content
  - **News**: TV 2 News content
  - **Catch-up**: 7-day catch-up for TV 2 channels
  - **Live streaming**: Live streams of TV 2 channels
- **Key Features**:
  - Freemium model (free tier + premium tier)
  - Diverse offerings (D2C, bundled packages, ad-supported)
  - Sports content
  - Danish content focus
  - Strategic initiatives contributing to competitive position
- **Platforms**: Web, mobile apps, smart TV apps, streaming devices
- **Market Position**: Leading commercial OTT service (matches Netflix in Denmark)
- **Revenue Model**: Advertising (free tier) + subscription (premium tier) + bundles

### 6.3 Regulatory Impact

- **License Fee**: Broadcasting fee funds public service broadcasters
- **Content Quotas**: European content quotas apply
