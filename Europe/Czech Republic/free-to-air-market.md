# Czech Republic Free-to-Air TV Market Analysis

## Executive Summary

Czech Republic's free-to-air TV market is experiencing a shift toward IPTV and cable (46% of households) vs terrestrial (41%), reflecting consumer preference for interactive services. The market is characterized by strong public service broadcasters (ČT), major commercial broadcasters (TV Nova, TV Prima), and a growing ecosystem of local OTT services that hero Czech content.

---

## 1. Free-to-Air Platform Distribution

### 1.1 Platform Breakdown (2025)

**Total TV Households: Data not publicly disclosed**

#### • IPTV and Cable Reception (Free-to-Air Tier)
- **Households**: 46% of households (2025)
- **Year-on-year change**: Growing (overtook terrestrial in 2024)
- **Primary delivery method**: IPTV and DOCSIS cable networks
- **Coverage**: Urban and suburban areas
- **Key characteristics**:
  - Basic cable/IPTV tier includes free-to-air channels
  - Interactive services (catch-up viewing, ad-skipping)
  - Most popular delivery method (overtook terrestrial)
  - Consumer preference for interactive features

#### • Digital Terrestrial Television (DTT)
- **Households**: 41% of households (2025)
- **Year-on-year change**: Declining (overtaken by IPTV/cable)
- **Primary delivery method**: DVB-T2 terrestrial network
- **Coverage**: Nationwide
- **Key characteristics**:
  - Free-to-air HD channels
  - No subscription required
  - Cost-effective alternative
  - Declining due to lack of interactive features

#### • Satellite Reception
- **Households**: 13% of households (2025)
- **Primary delivery method**: Satellite
- **Coverage**: Near universal
- **Key characteristics**: 
  - Free-to-air channels available without subscription
  - HD channels available
  - Declining usage

### 1.2 Platform Trends

**Key Developments:**
- **IPTV/Cable Growth**: Overtook terrestrial (46% vs 41%)
- **Terrestrial Decline**: Declining due to lack of interactive features
- **Satellite Decline**: Declining usage (13% of households)
- **Interactive Services**: Consumer preference for catch-up viewing and ad-skipping

**Market Shift:**
- IPTV and cable became predominant (46%) vs terrestrial (41%) in 2024
- Shift attributed to interactive services (catch-up viewing, ad-skipping)
- Five new nationwide free-to-air channels introduced in past 15 months

## 2. Main Free-to-Air Broadcasters

### 2.1 Public Service Broadcasters

#### • **ČT (Česká televize)**
- **Market Share**: 30.43% (2024, 15+ age group), 29.29% (H1 2025)
- **Position**: #1 broadcaster overall
- **Year-on-year change**: +0.55 percentage points (2024)
- **Main Channels**:
  - **ČT1** - Main national channel
  - **ČT2** - Culture and documentaries
  - **ČT24** - News channel
  - **ČT Sport** - Sports channel
  - **ČT :D** - Kids channel
  - **ČT Art** - Cultural channel
- **Reach**: Near universal coverage (public service broadcaster)
- **Content Focus**: News, entertainment, documentaries, cultural content, sports, kids content
- **Funding**: License fee + advertising
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Market leader (30.43% in 2024)
  - Strong news and information programming
  - Sports rights (domestic hockey championship drove 2024 growth)
  - High-quality documentaries
  - Cultural content
  - Kids programming
  - Must-carry on all platforms

### 2.2 Commercial Broadcasters

#### • **Nova Group (TV Nova)**
- **Market Share**: 26.88% (2024, 15+ age group)
- **Position**: #2 broadcaster overall
- **Year-on-year change**: Significant growth in H1 2025, particularly among younger viewers
- **Main Channels**:
  - **TV Nova** - Main commercial channel
  - **Nova Cinema** - Movies
  - **Nova Action** - Action content
  - **Nova Gold** - Classic content
- **Reach**: Leading commercial broadcaster
- **Content Focus**: Entertainment, series, reality TV, news
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Prime-time dominance (15-54 and 18-69 age groups)
  - Strong entertainment programming
  - Local content production
  - Series acquisitions
- **OTT Service**: **VOYO** (merged with O2 TV to form Oneplay in March 2025)

#### • **Prima Group (TV Prima)**
- **Market Share**: 26.83% (2024, 15+ age group)
- **Position**: #3 broadcaster overall
- **Main Channels**:
  - **Prima** - Main commercial channel
  - **Prima COOL** - Entertainment
  - **Prima MAX** - Movies
  - **Prima Krimi** - Crime content
- **Reach**: Major commercial broadcaster
- **Content Focus**: Entertainment, series, movies
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Strong entertainment programming
  - Local content production
  - Series and movie acquisitions

## 6. Market Dynamics & Trends

### 6.1 Platform Migration
- **IPTV/Cable Growth**: Overtook terrestrial (46% vs 41%)
- **Terrestrial Decline**: Declining due to lack of interactive features
- **Satellite Decline**: Declining usage (13%)
- **Interactive Services**: Consumer preference for catch-up viewing and ad-skipping

### 6.2 Content Consumption Trends
- **Interactive Services**: Consumer preference for catch-up viewing and ad-skipping
- **Pay-TV Pricing**: Average monthly expenditure CZK 424 (2024, +7% YoY, first time exceeding CZK 400)
- **Free-to-Air Shift**: One-third of viewers opted for free-to-air DTT due to pay-TV price increases
- **New Channels**: Five new nationwide free-to-air channels introduced in past 15 months

### 6.3 Regulatory Impact
- **Must-Carry Rules**: ČT must be carried on all platforms
- **License Fee**: Broadcasting fee funds public service broadcasters
- **Content Quotas**: European content quotas apply

## 7. Competitive Landscape

### 7.1 Free-to-Air vs Pay-TV
- **Free-to-Air**: Significant portion of households
- **Pay-TV**: 76.3% of households subscribe to paid television
- **Trend**: Pay-TV price increases (CZK 424/month) led to shift to free-to-air DTT

### 7.2 OTT Competition
- **Public Service OTT**: ČT iVysílání (free service)
- **Commercial OTT**: Oneplay (VOYO + O2 TV merger)
- **International OTT**: Netflix (28% market share), Amazon Prime Video (22%), SkyShowtime (4%)

### 7.3 Platform Competition
- **IPTV/Cable**: Most popular (46% of households)
- **DTT**: Second most popular (41% of households)
- **Satellite**: Declining (13% of households)
- **Internet**: Growing interactive consumption
