# TV Platform Operator: Telenet

## 1. Executive Summary

- **Overview of the operator's business**: Telenet is Belgium's largest cable and telecommunications operator, offering TV, broadband internet, mobile, and fixed-line services. The company operates primarily in Flanders (Dutch-speaking region) with extensive cable network coverage.

- **Key market position**: #1 Cable/Telco operator in Belgium, major market player in Flanders

- **Geographic footprint**: Primarily Flanders region, Belgium (cable network coverage)

- **Ownership structure**: Part of Telenet Group (Belgian telecommunications company)

- **Overall growth/decline assessment**: Challenged - Q1 2025 revenue +2.7% YoY (€721.2 million), but lost 2,100 fixed internet customers and 3,700 mobile subscribers. Revenue growth despite subscriber losses indicates ARPU improvements.

## 4. Growth Assessment

### 4.1 Market Position

- Ranking vs. competitors: #1 Cable/Telco operator in Belgium, major player in Flanders
- Strength in broadband, mobile, and TV: Strong in cable TV and broadband, mobile presence
- Differentiators: Extensive cable network, multi-play bundles, Flanders market leadership

### 4.2 Growth Indicators

- Subscriber trends across FTTH, broadband, TV, and mobile: Subscriber losses (2,100 fixed internet, 3,700 mobile in Q1 2025)
- ARPU trends: Revenue growth (+2.7%) despite subscriber losses indicates ARPU improvements
- Network upgrades: Fiber expansion, cable network upgrades
- New service launches: Enhanced broadband services, streaming integration

### 4.3 Decline Indicators

- Cord-cutting pressure: Moderate pressure from OTT competitors
- Regulatory or pricing pressure: Standard regulatory environment
- Content rights losses: Standard content portfolio maintained
- Competitive erosion from OTT: Competition from Netflix, Disney+, Amazon Prime Video, but also offers integrated services

### **Overall Assessment**

- **Operator Status:** Challenged / Stable

- **Drivers Behind the Trend:** Revenue growth +2.7% YoY in Q1 2025 (€721.2 million) despite subscriber losses (2,100 fixed internet, 3,700 mobile), indicating ARPU improvements and pricing power. However, subscriber losses across services indicate competitive challenges. Belgium's cable penetration >95% provides strong market foundation, but competition from Voo and OTT services creates pressure. Revenue growth demonstrates ability to maintain financial performance despite subscriber challenges.
