# TV Platform Operator: Ziggo (VodafoneZiggo)

## 1. Executive Summary

- **Overview of the operator's business**: Ziggo, now operating as VodafoneZiggo following merger, is the largest cable and telecommunications operator in the Netherlands, offering TV, broadband internet, mobile, and fixed-line services. The company operates cable and IPTV services across the Netherlands.

- **Key market position**: #1 Cable/Telco operator in the Netherlands, ~3.5 million video customers (March 2024), ~3.2 million broadband subscribers

- **Geographic footprint**: Nationwide coverage in the Netherlands

- **Ownership structure**: Part of VodafoneZiggo (joint venture between Vodafone and Liberty Global)

- **Overall growth/decline assessment**: Challenged - Revenue stable at €4.1 billion (2024), but video subscribers declining (-36,600 in Q4 2024, -18,500 broadband in Q3 2025). Q3 2025 revenue -3.9% YoY (€990 million).

### 4.3 Decline Indicators

- Cord-cutting pressure: Significant pressure from OTT competitors
- Regulatory or pricing pressure: Standard regulatory environment
- Content rights losses: Standard content portfolio maintained
- Competitive erosion from OTT: Competition from Netflix, Disney+, Amazon Prime Video, but also offers integrated services

### **Overall Assessment**

- **Operator Status:** Decline / Challenged

- **Drivers Behind the Trend:** Revenue stable at €4.1 billion (2024), achieving financial targets, but subscriber losses indicate challenges. Video subscribers declining (-36,600 in Q4 2024), broadband subscribers declining (-18,500 in Q3 2025). Q3 2025 revenue -3.9% YoY (€990 million) confirms revenue pressure. Largest cable operator position provides market leadership, but subscriber losses and revenue decline indicate competitive challenges. VodafoneZiggo integration provides scale advantages but faces OTT competition.
