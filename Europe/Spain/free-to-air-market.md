# Spain Free-to-Air TV Market Analysis

## Executive Summary

Spain's free-to-air TV market serves **~10-11 million households (55% of TV households)**, making it a significant segment of the Spanish TV market. The market is characterized by strong public service broadcaster (RTVE), major commercial broadcasters (Atresmedia, Mediaset España), and a growing ecosystem of local OTT services that hero Spanish content.

---

## 1. Free-to-Air Platform Distribution

### 1.1 Platform Breakdown (2024-2025)

**Total Free-to-Air Households: ~10-11 million (55% of ~19.35 million TV households)**

#### • IPTV/FTTH Reception
- **Households**: ~6.8 million (35% of all TV households, 2024)
- **Primary delivery method**: IPTV, FTTH networks (Movistar, Vodafone, Orange, MásMóvil)
- **Coverage**: High FTTH coverage (~85% availability)
- **Key characteristics**: 
  - Fastest growing platform
  - High broadband penetration (~95% of households)
  - FTTH availability: ~85% (high coverage)
  - Interactive services preferred
  - Hybrid STB penetration: ~50%
  - OTT integration common

#### • Satellite Reception
- **Households**: ~5.8 million (30% of all TV households, 2024)
- **Primary delivery method**: Hispasat, Eutelsat satellites
- **Coverage**: Near universal (98%+)
- **Key characteristics**:
  - Free-to-air channels available without subscription
  - HD/4K enhancement available
  - CI+ CAM modules supported (~45% of pay-TV households)
  - Strong presence in rural areas
  - Fransat platform (French satellite) also available

#### • Digital Terrestrial Television (DTT)
- **Households**: ~3.9 million (20% of all TV households, 2024)
- **Primary delivery method**: DVB-T2 terrestrial network
- **Coverage**: Nationwide (98%+ population coverage)
- **Key characteristics**:
  - Free-to-air HD channels (RTVE, Atresmedia, Mediaset España)
  - No subscription required
  - MUX1-8 spectrum allocation
  - DVB-T2 transmission technology approved (March 2025)
  - UHD broadcasts enabled (RTVE: 2 UHD channels, Atresmedia: 1 UHD channel, Mediaset España: 1 UHD channel)
  - New nationwide DTT channel license expected
  - Six DTT licenses renewed for 15 years (June 2025) - Atresmedia, Mediaset, Veo TV, Net TV

#### • Cable Reception (Free-to-Air Tier)
- **Households**: ~2.9 million (15% of all TV households, 2024)
- **Primary delivery method**: DOCSIS cable networks
- **Coverage**: Urban and suburban areas
- **Key characteristics**:
  - Basic cable tier includes free-to-air channels
  - Must-carry rules ensure RTVE availability
  - Regional operators (Euskaltel, etc.)

#### • Internet-Based Reception (IPTV/OTT)
- **Households**: ~15% OTT-only households (2024)
- **Primary delivery method**: IPTV, streaming apps, smart TV apps
- **Key characteristics**:
  - Growing trend toward internet-based consumption
  - Includes both free-to-air catch-up services and paid streaming
  - Smart TV penetration: ~75% (preferred device for streaming)
  - Streaming media players: ~45% penetration
  - **95% of internet users (16-75) access TV content online** (2024)
  - **61.9% of households with internet use streaming platforms** (2024)
  - Hybrid consumption (linear + streaming)

### 1.2 Platform Trends

**Key Developments:**
- **IPTV/FTTH**: Fastest growing platform (35% of households, high FTTH coverage ~85%)
- **Satellite**: Stable presence (30% of households)
- **DTT**: Stable with UHD upgrade (20% of households, DVB-T2 approved March 2025)
- **Internet**: Strong OTT consumption (15% OTT-only households, 95% of internet users access TV online)

**Market Dynamics:**
- High smart TV penetration (~75%, preferred device for streaming)
- Strong OTT adoption (61.9% of households with internet use streaming platforms)
- UHD rollout on DTT (RTVE: 2 UHD channels, Atresmedia: 1 UHD channel, Mediaset España: 1 UHD channel)
- DTT licenses renewed for 15 years (June 2025)
- TV advertising revenue decline: -8.3% H1 2025 vs H1 2024 (linear TV)

## 2. Main Free-to-Air Broadcasters

### 2.1 Public Service Broadcaster

#### • **RTVE (Radiotelevisión Española)**
- **Market Share**: Leading public service broadcaster
- **Position**: #1 or #2 broadcaster overall (varies by time slot)
- **Structure**: Public service broadcaster (state-owned)
- **Main Channels**:
  - **La 1** - Main national channel (news, entertainment, drama)
  - **La 2** - Cultural, documentaries, educational content
  - **Canal 24 Horas** - 24-hour news channel
  - **Clan** - Children's content
  - **Teledeporte** - Sports content
  - **UHD Channels**: 2 UHD channels (approved March 2025)
- **Reach**: Near universal coverage (public service broadcaster)
- **Content Focus**: News, entertainment, documentaries, cultural content, sports, regional content
- **Funding**: Public funding + advertising (limited)
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Must-carry on all platforms
  - Strong news and information programming
  - High-quality documentaries and cultural content
  - Regional content through territorial disconnections
  - Strong sports coverage (La Liga highlights, basketball, tennis)
  - UHD leadership (2 UHD channels on DTT)

### 2.2 Commercial Broadcasters

#### • **Atresmedia**
- **Market Share**: Significant free-to-air and pay-TV presence
- **Position**: #2 or #3 broadcaster overall
- **Structure**: Commercial broadcaster
- **Main Channels**:
  - **Antena 3** - Main entertainment channel (reality TV, series, movies)
  - **laSexta** - Entertainment, news, series
  - **Neox** - Youth-oriented content
  - **Nova** - Women-oriented content
  - **Mega** - General entertainment
  - **Atreseries** - Series content
  - **UHD Channel**: 1 UHD channel (approved March 2025)
- **Reach**: Nationwide coverage
- **Content Focus**: Entertainment, reality TV, series, movies, news
- **Funding**: Advertising revenue
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Strong commercial broadcaster
  - Popular reality TV formats
  - Spanish series and movies
  - Strong advertising presence
  - Landmark Disney+ partnership (September 2025): 300+ hours of Spanish content annually
  - Popular series: "Money Heist" ("Casa de papel"), "Veneno," "Gran Hotel"

#### • **Mediaset España**
- **Market Share**: Significant free-to-air presence
- **Position**: #3 or #4 broadcaster overall
- **Structure**: Commercial broadcaster (Mediaset group)
- **Main Channels**:
  - **Telecinco** - Main entertainment channel (reality TV, series, movies)
  - **Cuatro** - Entertainment, news, series
  - **Divinity** - Women-oriented content
  - **Energy** - Youth-oriented content
  - **Be Mad** - General entertainment
  - **UHD Channel**: 1 UHD channel (approved March 2025)
- **Reach**: Nationwide coverage
- **Content Focus**: Entertainment, reality TV, series, movies, news
- **Funding**: Advertising revenue
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Strong commercial broadcaster
  - Popular reality TV formats
  - Spanish series and movies
  - Strong advertising presence

## 3. Local OTT Services (Heroing Spanish Content)

### 3.1 Public Service Broadcaster OTT Services

#### • **RTVE Play**
- **Owner**: RTVE (public service broadcaster)
- **Subscribers**: Free service (publicly funded)
- **Monthly Users**: 10.5 million unique viewers (October 2024)
- **Market Position**: Leading Spanish OTT platform (October 2024)
- **Content Focus**:
  - **Information**: News, documentaries, cultural content
  - **Entertainment**: RTVE series, movies, entertainment
  - **Catch-up**: 7-day catch-up for RTVE channels
  - **Live streaming**: Live streams of RTVE channels (La 1, La 2, Canal 24 Horas, Clan, Teledeporte)
  - **Regional content**: Territorial disconnections available
- **Key Features**:
  - Free access (no subscription)
  - Extensive catch-up library
  - High-quality documentaries and news
  - Spanish content focus
  - **27% of TV consumption in Spain** (October 2024)
  - Strategic partnership with Telefónica Audiovisual Digital (February 2024): All RTVE channels distributed on Movistar Plus+ including territorial disconnections
- **Platforms**: Web, mobile apps, smart TV apps, streaming devices
- **Market Position**: Leading Spanish OTT platform (October 2024)

### 3.2 Commercial Broadcaster OTT Services

#### • **Atresplayer**
- **Owner**: Atresmedia (commercial broadcaster)
- **Subscribers**: Freemium model (free tier + premium tier)
- **Content Focus**:
  - **Local Content**: Reality TV shows, fictional series, Spanish productions
  - **Entertainment**: Atresmedia content, series, movies
  - **Original Content**: Atresplayer Originals
  - **Sports**: Selected sports content
- **Key Features**:
  - Freemium model (free tier + premium tier)
  - Extensive Spanish content library
  - Original productions
  - **Landmark Disney+ partnership (September 2025)**: 300+ hours of Spanish content annually, dedicated Atresplayer-branded section within Disney+ app
  - Popular series: "Money Heist" ("Casa de papel"), "Veneno," "Gran Hotel"
- **Platforms**: Web, mobile apps, smart TV apps, streaming devices
- **Market Position**: Leading commercial Spanish OTT service
- **Revenue Model**: Advertising (free tier) + subscription (premium tier)

#### • **Movistar Plus+**
- **Owner**: Movistar (Telefónica)
- **Subscribers**: Paid subscription service (~4-5 million subscribers)
- **Content Focus**:
  - **Local Content**: Spanish series, movies, entertainment
  - **Original Productions**: 28 original titles (2023/2024 season, leading ranking)
  - **Entertainment**: Movistar Plus+ content library
  - **Sports**: La Liga rights, basketball, tennis
- **Key Features**:
  - Paid subscription service
  - **Leading original productions**: 28 titles in 2023/2024 season
  - Strategic partnership with RTVE (February 2024): All RTVE channels distributed on Movistar Plus+ including territorial disconnections
  - Multi-platform access (satellite, IPTV, OTT)
  - 4K, HDR support
- **Platforms**: Web, mobile apps, smart TV apps, streaming devices, Movistar STBs
- **Market Position**: Leading pay-TV and OTT service
- **Revenue Model**: Subscription

## 4. Market Insights

### 4.1 Free-to-Air Platform Preferences
1. **IPTV/FTTH** fastest growing (35% of households, high FTTH coverage ~85%)
2. **Satellite** stable (30% of households)
3. **DTT** stable with UHD upgrade (20% of households, DVB-T2 approved March 2025)
4. **Internet** strong OTT consumption (15% OTT-only households, 95% of internet users access TV online)

### 4.2 Broadcaster Market Shares
1. **RTVE**: Leading public service broadcaster (must-carry, strong news/information, UHD leadership)
2. **Atresmedia**: Leading commercial broadcaster (significant free-to-air and pay-TV presence, Disney+ partnership)
3. **Mediaset España**: Strong commercial broadcaster (significant free-to-air presence)
4. **Movistar Plus+**: Leading pay-TV operator (~4-5M subscribers, ~25% pay-TV market share)

### 4.3 Local OTT Services
1. **RTVE Play**: Leading Spanish OTT platform (10.5M unique viewers, October 2024, 27% of TV consumption)
2. **Atresplayer**: Leading commercial OTT (freemium model, Disney+ partnership)
3. **Movistar Plus+**: Leading pay-TV OTT (28 original titles, 2023/2024 season)
4. **OTT Adoption**: 61.9% of households with internet use streaming platforms (2024)

### 4.4 Content Strategy
- **Public Service**: Information/news focus, cultural content, regional content (territorial disconnections), UHD leadership (2 UHD channels)
- **Commercial**: Entertainment focus (reality TV, series), strong advertising presence, international partnerships (Atresmedia-Disney+)
- **Local Content**: Very high preference for Spanish content
- **Original Productions**: Movistar Plus+ leading with 28 original titles (2023/2024 season)
- **Streaming Trend**: 95% of internet users access TV content online, 61.9% of households use streaming platforms

## 5. Regulatory Environment

### 5.1 Must-Carry Rules
- **RTVE**: Must be carried on all platforms (public service broadcaster)
- **Regulatory Authority**: CNMC (Comisión Nacional de los Mercados y la Competencia)
- **Enforcement**: Mandatory for all TV platforms

### 5.2 Content Quotas
- **European Content Quotas**: Apply to all broadcasters
- **Local Content**: Strong preference for Spanish content
- **Dubbing Requirements**: Spanish dubbing preferred, subtitling common

### 5.3 Platform Regulation
- **DTT Spectrum**: MUX1-8 allocation
- **DVB-T2 Technology**: Approved March 2025, enables UHD broadcasts
- **DTT Licenses**: Six licenses renewed for 15 years (June 2025) - Atresmedia, Mediaset, Veo TV, Net TV
- **UHD Channels**: RTVE (2 UHD channels), Atresmedia (1 UHD channel), Mediaset España (1 UHD channel)
- **New DTT Channel**: Nationwide DTT channel license expected
- **CI+ Certification**: Required via CI+ Consortium

## 6. Competitive Landscape

### 6.1 Free-to-Air vs Pay-TV
- **Free-to-Air**: ~10-11 million households (55% of TV households)
- **Pay-TV**: ~8-9 million households (45% of TV households)
- **Trend**: OTT growing (61.9% of households with internet use streaming platforms), but free-to-air remains significant

### 6.2 OTT Competition
- **Public Service OTT**: RTVE Play (10.5M unique viewers, October 2024, 27% of TV consumption)
- **Commercial OTT**: Atresplayer (Disney+ partnership), Movistar Plus+ (28 original titles)
- **International OTT**: Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, Disney+, HBO Max
- **OTT Adoption**: 61.9% of households with internet use streaming platforms (2024)

### 6.3 Platform Competition
- **IPTV/FTTH**: Fastest growing (35% of households, high FTTH coverage ~85%)
- **Satellite**: Stable (30% of households)
- **DTT**: Stable with UHD upgrade (20% of households, DVB-T2 approved March 2025)
- **Internet**: Strong OTT consumption (15% OTT-only households, 95% of internet users access TV online)

### 6.4 Advertising Market
- **TV Advertising Revenue**: ~€2.2 billion (2024)
- **Linear TV Decline**: -8.3% H1 2025 vs H1 2024
- **Atresmedia & Mediaset España**: Combined -9% ad revenue decline H1 2025 (€688.9M total)
- **AVOD/CTV Ad Spend**: ~€500 million
- **Programmatic Adoption**: ~42%

## 7. Key Insights

### 7.1 Market Characteristics
- **Free-to-Air Significance**: 55% of TV households are free-to-air
- **IPTV/FTTH Leadership**: Fastest growing platform (35% of households, high FTTH coverage ~85%)
- **Strong Local Content**: Very high preference for Spanish content
- **Public Service Strength**: RTVE leading with strong news/information and UHD leadership (2 UHD channels)

### 7.2 Growth Opportunities
- **Smart TV Penetration**: ~75% (preferred device for streaming)

- **UHD Rollout**: DVB-T2 approved (March 2025), UHD channels launched (RTVE: 2, Atresmedia: 1, Mediaset España: 1)
- **RTVE Play Success**: 10.5M unique viewers (October 2024), 27% of TV consumption in Spain
- **Original Productions**: Movistar Plus+ leading with 28 original titles (2023/2024 season)

### 7.3 Strategic Considerations
- **Must-Carry Compliance**: RTVE must be carried on all platforms
- **Local Content Focus**: Spanish content preferred, strong original productions
- **Streaming Integration**: Growing importance of OTT services (RTVE Play, Atresplayer, Movistar Plus+)
- **International Partnerships**: Atresmedia-Disney+ partnership (September 2025), RTVE-Movistar Plus+ partnership (February 2024)
- **UHD Technology**: DVB-T2 enables UHD broadcasts, UHD channels launched
