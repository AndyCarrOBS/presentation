# TV Platform Operator: HD+

## 1. Executive Summary

- **Overview of the operator's business**: HD+ is a German TV platform that offers high-definition (HD) and Ultra-HD (UHD) content via satellite and IPTV. HD+ is a subsidiary of SES (Satellite operator) and was founded in 2009. The platform operates on a "pay-lite" model, providing premium HD/UHD content on top of free-to-air channels.

- **Key market position**: #3 Pay-TV platform in Germany, ~15% pay-TV market share, ~2.1 million direct household customers (2024), with additional customers accessing HD+ content via Sky. Approximately 16.5 million households in Germany receive TV via satellite.

- **Geographic footprint**: Nationwide coverage in Germany via satellite (Astra satellite system)

- **Ownership structure**: Subsidiary of SES (Société Européenne des Satellites), Luxembourg-based satellite operator. HD PLUS GmbH is the operating company.

- **Overall growth/decline assessment**: Stable - maintaining subscriber base with focus on premium HD/UHD content. Strategic repositioning in 2025 with closer cooperation with SES to adapt to changing TV consumption habits.

## 4. Growth Assessment

### 4.1 Market Position

- Ranking vs. competitors: #3 Pay-TV platform, ~15% pay-TV market share
- Strength in broadband, mobile, and TV: Strong in premium HD/UHD satellite TV content
- Differentiators: Pay-lite model (affordable premium HD/UHD content), satellite delivery provides universal coverage, partnership with SES for satellite infrastructure, content also distributed via Sky platform

### 4.2 Growth Indicators

- Subscriber trends across FTTH, broadband, TV, and mobile: Stable direct subscriber base (~2.1 million), additional reach via Sky platform
- ARPU trends: Data not publicly disclosed
- Network upgrades: Enhanced HD/UHD content offering, IPTV expansion
- New service launches: Closer cooperation with SES announced September 2025, strategic repositioning to adapt to changing TV consumption habits

### 4.3 Decline Indicators

- Cord-cutting pressure: Moderate pressure from OTT competitors
- Regulatory or pricing pressure: Standard regulatory environment
- Content rights losses: Standard content portfolio maintained
- Competitive erosion from OTT: Competition from Netflix, Disney+, Amazon Prime Video, but pay-lite model provides affordability advantage

### **Overall Assessment**

- **Operator Status:** Stable

- **Drivers Behind the Trend:** HD+ maintains stable position with ~2.1 million direct customers and additional reach via Sky platform. The pay-lite model provides affordable access to premium HD/UHD content, differentiating from full pay-TV packages. Strategic repositioning in September 2025 with closer SES cooperation aims to adapt to changing TV consumption habits. Satellite delivery provides universal coverage advantage. However, limited public disclosure of financial metrics makes detailed assessment challenging.
