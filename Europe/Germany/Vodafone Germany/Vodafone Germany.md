# TV Platform Operator: Vodafone Germany

## 1. Executive Summary

- **Overview of the operator's business**: Vodafone Germany is the largest cable and telecommunications operator in Germany, offering TV, broadband, mobile, and fixed-line services. The company operates a hybrid fiber cable network covering over 24 million households, making it the largest provider of fixed-line gigabit connectivity in Germany.

- **Key market position**: #1 Cable/Telco operator in Germany, ~25% pay-TV market share, ~8.9 million TV subscribers, largest gigabit network provider

- **Geographic footprint**: Nationwide coverage in Germany, with strong presence in former Unitymedia territories (North Rhine-Westphalia, Hesse, Baden-Württemberg)

- **Ownership structure**: Part of Vodafone Group plc (UK-listed multinational telecommunications company)

- **Overall growth/decline assessment**: Facing challenges due to regulatory changes (MDU TV transition), but showing signs of recovery with broadband growth and strategic investments in gigabit network

## 4. Growth Assessment

### 4.1 Market Position

- Ranking vs. competitors: #1 Cable/Telco operator, largest gigabit network provider in Germany
- Strength in broadband, mobile, and TV: Strong in cable TV and broadband, growing mobile presence, largest gigabit network coverage
- Differentiators: Largest gigabit network (24+ million households), quad-play bundles, Unitymedia integration provides scale

### 4.2 Growth Indicators

- Subscriber trends across FTTH, broadband, TV, and mobile: Broadband showing recovery, mobile contract growth, TV stabilizing post-MDU transition
- ARPU trends: Mobile ARPU stable, contract ARPU higher than prepaid
- Network upgrades: Significant investment in gigabit network expansion, hybrid fiber cable network
- New service launches: Gigabit internet services, enhanced TV platform, streaming integration

### 4.3 Decline Indicators

- Cord-cutting pressure: Moderate pressure from OTT competitors
- Regulatory or pricing pressure: Significant impact from MDU TV regulatory changes (July 2024), termination of bulk TV contracts
- Content rights losses: Standard content portfolio maintained
- Competitive erosion from OTT: Competition from Netflix, Disney+, Amazon Prime Video, but also offers integrated OTT services

### **Overall Assessment**

- **Operator Status:** Recovery / Stable (post-regulatory impact)

- **Drivers Behind the Trend:** Regulatory changes in July 2024 (MDU TV transition) significantly impacted revenue and subscriber base, causing 6.0% revenue decline in FY2025. However, company is showing signs of recovery with broadband stability returning in second half of FY2025, mobile contract growth, and strategic focus on gigabit network expansion. Unitymedia acquisition provides scale and network coverage advantages.
