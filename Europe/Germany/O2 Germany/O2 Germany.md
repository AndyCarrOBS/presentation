# TV Platform Operator: O2 Germany (Telefónica Germany)

## 1. Executive Summary

- **Overview of the operator's business**: O2 Germany (operated by Telefónica Germany) is a major telecommunications operator in Germany, primarily focused on mobile and fixed-line services. The company offers TV services as part of its service portfolio, though TV is not the primary focus compared to mobile and broadband services.

- **Key market position**: Major mobile operator in Germany, TV services are secondary to mobile and fixed-line operations. Mobile-focused operator with growing fixed-line presence.

- **Geographic footprint**: Nationwide coverage in Germany

- **Ownership structure**: Part of Telefónica Group (Spanish multinational telecommunications company), operating under the O2 brand in Germany

- **Overall growth/decline assessment**: Growing in mobile and fixed-line segments - Strong mobile contract customer growth (824,000 net additions in 2024, 164,000 in Q1 2025, 184,000 in Q2 2025), fixed-line revenue growth (+3.6% in 2024). TV services data not publicly disclosed, suggesting smaller market presence compared to primary TV operators.

## 4. Growth Assessment

### 4.1 Market Position

- Ranking vs. competitors: Major mobile operator, smaller TV market presence compared to primary TV operators (Vodafone, Deutsche Telekom, Sky)
- Strength in broadband, mobile, and TV: Strong in mobile services, growing fixed-line presence, TV services secondary
- Differentiators: Mobile-first approach, competitive mobile pricing, family contracts, converged service offerings

### 4.2 Growth Indicators

- Subscriber trends across FTTH, broadband, TV, and mobile: Strong mobile contract growth, fixed-line revenue growth, TV data not disclosed
- ARPU trends: Mobile ARPU declined (-3.4%) due to regulatory changes (mobile termination rates) and family contract mix
- Network upgrades: Fixed-line network expansion, fiber investments
- New service launches: Enhanced mobile services, fixed-line expansion, converged offerings

### 4.3 Decline Indicators

- Cord-cutting pressure: Not primary concern (TV not core business)
- Regulatory or pricing pressure: Mobile termination rate reductions impacted mobile ARPU
- Content rights losses: Not primary concern (TV not core business)
- Competitive erosion from OTT: Limited impact (TV not core business)

### **Overall Assessment**

- **Operator Status:** Growth (in mobile and fixed-line segments)

- **Drivers Behind the Trend:** O2 Germany is primarily a mobile operator with TV services as a secondary offering. Strong mobile contract customer growth (824,000 net additions in 2024, continuing in 2025) demonstrates competitive mobile positioning. Fixed-line business showing growth (+3.6% revenue in 2024) driven by fiber and cable demand. Mobile ARPU decline (-3.4%) due to regulatory changes (50% reduction in mobile termination rates) and increased share of secondary SIM cards in family contracts. TV services data not publicly disclosed, suggesting smaller market presence compared to primary TV operators. Company's focus is on mobile and fixed-line growth rather than TV market leadership.
