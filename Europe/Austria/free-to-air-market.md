# Austria Free-to-Air TV Market Analysis

## Executive Summary

Austria's free-to-air TV market is dominated by satellite reception (53% of households), with cable declining and terrestrial minimal. The market is characterized by strong public service broadcasters (ORF), major commercial broadcasters (ATV, Puls 4, ServusTV), and a growing ecosystem of local OTT services that hero Austrian content.

---

## 1. Free-to-Air Platform Distribution

### 1.1 Platform Breakdown (2024-2025)

**Total TV Households: Data not publicly disclosed**

#### • Satellite Reception
- **Households**: 53% of Austrian television households (4.027 million people aged 12+, 2024)
- **Year-on-year change**: +30,000 individuals (2024)
- **Primary delivery method**: Astra satellite (19.2° East)
- **Coverage**: Near universal (98%+)
- **Key characteristics**: 
  - Free-to-air channels available without subscription
  - HD channels available
  - Most popular delivery method
  - ORF and ORS Group extended contract with SES (November 2024)
  - SES and RTL Deutschland extended partnership (July 2024)

#### • Cable Reception (Free-to-Air Tier)
- **Households**: 42% of population (3.164 million people aged 12+, 2024)
- **Year-on-year change**: -31,000 individuals (2024)
- **Primary delivery method**: DOCSIS cable networks and IPTV
- **Coverage**: Urban and suburban areas
- **Key characteristics**:
  - Basic cable/IPTV tier includes free-to-air channels
  - Must-carry rules ensure ORF availability
  - Declining usage

#### • Digital Terrestrial Television (DTT)
- **Households**: 5% of television households (205,000 households, 2024)
- **Year-on-year change**: -19,000 households (2024)
- **Primary delivery method**: DVB-T/DVB-T2 terrestrial network
- **Coverage**: Nationwide
- **Key characteristics**:
  - Free-to-air HD channels
  - No subscription required
  - Minimal usage (5% of households)
  - Declining

### 1.2 Platform Trends

**Key Developments:**
- **Satellite**: Dominant and growing (+30,000 individuals)
- **Cable**: Declining (-31,000 individuals)
- **DTT**: Minimal usage (5%) and declining (-19,000 households)
- **Partnerships**: Extended satellite partnerships (ORF/ORS-SES, RTL Deutschland-SES)

## 2. Main Free-to-Air Broadcasters

### 2.1 Public Service Broadcasters

#### • **ORF (Österreichischer Rundfunk)**
- **Market Share**: 34.8% overall (November 2025), ORF 2: 20.9% (2024), ORF 1: 11.4% (November 2025, up from 10.1% in 2024)
- **Position**: #1 broadcaster overall
- **Structure**: National public broadcaster
- **Main Channels**:
  - **ORF 1** - Main national channel (11.4% market share, November 2025)
  - **ORF 2** - Regional and information (20.9% market share, 2024)
  - **ORF III** - Culture and documentaries
  - **ORF Sport plus** - Sports channel
  - **ORF regional channels** - Regional content
- **Reach**: Near universal coverage (public service broadcaster)
- **Content Focus**: News, entertainment, documentaries, regional content, sports, cultural content
- **Funding**: License fee (GIS fee) + advertising (limited)
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Market leader (34.8% overall)
  - Strong news and information programming (ORF 2: 20.9%)
  - Sports rights (popular sports broadcasts drove November 2025 growth)
  - Regional content production
  - High-quality documentaries
  - Cultural content
  - Must-carry on all platforms
- **Satellite Partnership**: Extended contract with SES (November 2024) for Astra 19.2° East distribution

### 2.2 Commercial Broadcasters

#### • **ServusTV**
- **Market Share**: 5.3% (2024), 5.1% (November 2025)
- **Position**: #1 private broadcaster
- **Main Channels**:
  - **ServusTV** - Main commercial channel
- **Reach**: Major commercial broadcaster
- **Content Focus**: Entertainment, sports
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Leading private broadcaster (5.3% in 2024, 5.1% in November 2025)
  - Strong entertainment programming
  - Sports content
  - Stable market position

#### • **Puls 4**
- **Market Share**: 2.5% (2024), 5.3% (November 2025, 12-49 age group)
- **Position**: #2 private broadcaster (in 12-49 age group)
- **Year-on-year change**: Significant growth in 12-49 age group (5.3% vs ServusTV's 5.1%)
- **Main Channels**:
  - **Puls 4** - Main commercial channel
- **Reach**: Commercial broadcaster
- **Content Focus**: Entertainment
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Strong growth in 12-49 age group (5.3%, surpassing ServusTV)
  - Entertainment programming
  - Younger audience focus

#### • **ATV**
- **Market Share**: 2.5% (2024)
- **Position**: Commercial broadcaster
- **Main Channels**:
  - **ATV** - Main commercial channel
- **Reach**: Commercial broadcaster
- **Content Focus**: Entertainment, news
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Entertainment and news programming
  - Local content production

## 3. Local OTT Services (Heroing Austrian Content)

### 3.1 Public Service Broadcaster OTT Services

#### • **ORF TVthek**
- **Owner**: ORF (public service broadcaster)
- **Subscribers**: Free service (funded by GIS fee)
- **Content Focus**:
  - **Information**: News and information programming
  - **Entertainment**: ORF series, documentaries, regional content
  - **Sports**: ORF Sport plus content
  - **Catch-up**: 7-day catch-up for ORF channels
  - **Live streaming**: Live streams of ORF channels
  - **Regional Content**: Content from ORF regional channels
- **Key Features**:
  - Free access (no subscription)
  - Extensive catch-up library
  - High-quality documentaries and news
  - Austrian content focus
  - Regional content
- **Platforms**: Web, mobile apps, smart TV apps, streaming devices
- **Market Position**: Leading public service OTT service

### 3.2 Commercial Broadcaster OTT Services

- **Owner**: ServusTV
- **Subscribers**: Data not publicly disclosed
- **Content Focus**:

  - **Live streaming**: Live streams of ServusTV channels
- **Key Features**:
  - Freemium model (free tier + premium tier)

  - Austrian content focus
- **Platforms**: Web, mobile apps, smart TV apps, streaming devices
