# Switzerland Free-to-Air TV Market Analysis

## Executive Summary

Switzerland's free-to-air TV market is unique, having discontinued DTT services in June 2019. The market is dominated by internet-based TV (61% of households) and cable (32%), with satellite at 10%. The market is characterized by strong public service broadcasters (SRG SSR), and a growing ecosystem of local OTT services that hero Swiss content.

---

## 1. Free-to-Air Platform Distribution

### 1.1 Platform Breakdown (2025)

**Total TV Households: Data not publicly disclosed**

#### • Internet-Based Reception (IPTV/OTT)
- **Households**: 61% of households (2025)
- **Year-on-year change**: Stable (no significant change)
- **Primary delivery method**: IPTV, streaming apps, smart TV apps
- **Coverage**: Nationwide (broadband dependent)
- **Key characteristics**:
  - Dominant delivery method
  - Includes both free-to-air catch-up services and paid streaming
  - Interactive services (catch-up viewing, on-demand)
  - Growing trend

#### • Cable Reception (Free-to-Air Tier)
- **Households**: 32% of households (2025)
- **Year-on-year change**: Stable
- **Primary delivery method**: DOCSIS cable networks
- **Coverage**: Urban and suburban areas
- **Key characteristics**:
  - Basic cable tier includes free-to-air channels
  - Must-carry rules ensure SRG SSR availability
  - Second most popular delivery method

#### • Satellite Reception
- **Households**: 10% of households (2025)
- **Year-on-year change**: Stable (no significant change)
- **Primary delivery method**: Satellite (Astra, Eutelsat)
- **Coverage**: Near universal
- **Key characteristics**: 
  - Free-to-air channels available without subscription
  - HD channels available
  - Recommended for former DTT users (post-DTT shutdown)

#### • Digital Terrestrial Television (DTT)
- **Status**: **Discontinued June 2019**
- **Former Usage**: 64,000 households (1.9% of population)
- **Reason**: Low usage (only 1.9% of population)
- **Transition**: SRG SSR recommended satellite reception for former DTT users
- **Impact**: Minimal impact due to low usage

### 1.2 Platform Trends

**Key Developments:**
- **DTT Shutdown**: Discontinued June 2019 (low usage, 1.9% of population)
- **Internet Dominance**: 61% of households use internet-based TV (stable)
- **Cable Stable**: 32% of households (stable)
- **Satellite Stable**: 10% of households (stable, recommended for former DTT users)

**Unique Market Characteristics:**
- Only European country to fully discontinue DTT
- Internet-based TV dominant (61%)
- High cable penetration (32%)
- Low satellite usage (10%)

## 2. Main Free-to-Air Broadcasters

### 2.1 Public Service Broadcasters

#### • **SRG SSR (Schweizerische Radio- und Fernsehgesellschaft)**
- **Market Share**: Leading broadcaster
- **Position**: Public service broadcaster
- **Structure**: National public broadcaster (multi-language)
- **Main Channels**:
  - **SRF 1** - Main German-language channel
  - **SRF 2** - Culture and documentaries (German)
  - **RTS 1** - Main French-language channel
  - **RTS 2** - Culture and documentaries (French)
  - **RSI La 1** - Main Italian-language channel
  - **RSI La 2** - Culture and documentaries (Italian)
  - **RTR** - Romansh-language channel
- **Reach**: Near universal coverage (public service broadcaster)
- **Content Focus**: News, entertainment, documentaries, cultural content, multi-language content
- **Funding**: License fee (Billag/Serafe fee) + advertising (limited)
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Multi-language broadcaster (German, French, Italian, Romansh)
  - Strong news and information programming
  - Regional content production
  - High-quality documentaries
  - Cultural content
  - Must-carry on all platforms
- **OTT Strategy**: Consolidating regional streaming services (Play SRF, Play RTS, Play RSI, Play RTR, Play Suisse) into single multilingual platform by autumn 2025

### 2.2 Commercial Broadcasters

**Note**: Switzerland has limited commercial broadcasters due to strong public service broadcaster position. Most commercial content accessed via international channels or streaming.

## 3. Local OTT Services (Heroing Swiss Content)

### 3.1 Public Service Broadcaster OTT Services

#### • **SRG SSR Streaming Services (Consolidating)**
- **Owner**: SRG SSR (public service broadcaster)
- **Current Services**: Play SRF (German), Play RTS (French), Play RSI (Italian), Play RTR (Romansh), Play Suisse (national)
- **Future Service**: Single unified multilingual platform (autumn 2025)
- **Subscribers**: Free service (funded by Billag/Serafe fee)
- **Content Focus**:
  - **Information**: News and information programming (multi-language)
  - **Entertainment**: SRG SSR series, documentaries, regional content
  - **Catch-up**: Catch-up for SRG SSR channels
  - **Live streaming**: Live streams of SRG SSR channels
  - **Multi-language**: German, French, Italian, Romansh content
- **Key Features**:
  - Free access (no subscription)
  - Extensive catch-up library
  - High-quality documentaries and news
  - Swiss content focus
  - Multi-language content
  - **Consolidation**: Unified platform by autumn 2025
- **Platforms**: Web, mobile apps, smart TV apps, streaming devices
- **Market Position**: Leading public service OTT service

## 6. Market Dynamics & Trends

### 6.1 Platform Migration
- **DTT Shutdown**: Discontinued June 2019 (low usage)
- **Internet Growth**: Dominant (61% of households, stable)
- **Cable Stable**: 32% of households (stable)
- **Satellite Stable**: 10% of households (stable, recommended for former DTT users)

### 6.2 Content Consumption Trends
- **Catch-up Usage**: High usage of SRG SSR streaming services
- **Streaming Growth**: OTT market growing (+21.6% YoY, CHF 903M in 2024)
- **Hybrid Consumption**: Linear + streaming combination
- **Local Content Preference**: Strong preference for Swiss content (multi-language)

### 6.3 Regulatory Impact
- **Must-Carry Rules**: SRG SSR must be carried on all platforms
- **Billag/Serafe Fee**: Broadcasting fee funds public service broadcasters
- **Content Quotas**: European content quotas apply
- **DTT Shutdown**: Unique decision to discontinue DTT (June 2019)
