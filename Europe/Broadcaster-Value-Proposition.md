# TV OS Value Proposition for Local Broadcasters & OTT Platforms
## Solving Broadcaster Needs While Creating Mutual Value

---

## Executive Summary

This document identifies the critical needs of local broadcasters and OTT companies providing local content, and positions a new smart TV streaming OS as the solution that addresses these needs while creating significant value for both broadcasters and the OS platform itself.

**Core Value Proposition:**
A TV OS that **solves broadcaster pain points** (content discovery, distribution costs, audience reach, data insights, revenue generation) while **creating platform value** (content differentiation, user engagement, advertising revenue, market leadership).

**Mutual Value Creation:**
- **For Broadcasters**: Reduced costs, increased reach, better data, guaranteed prominence, simplified distribution
- **For TV OS**: Premium local content, user differentiation, advertising inventory, regulatory compliance, market leadership

## 1. Broadcaster & OTT Needs Analysis

### 1.1 Content Discovery & Prominence

**The Problem:**
- **Content Fragmentation**: 33% of users feel service fragmentation negatively impacts experience, 45% find streaming overwhelming
- **Algorithmic Deprioritization**: Smart TV platforms use proprietary algorithms that can deprioritize local content in favor of global streaming services
- **Competition with Global Giants**: Netflix, Disney+, Amazon Prime Video dominate smart TV home screens
- **Lack of Prominence**: Local broadcasters struggle to ensure their content is discoverable on smart TV platforms
- **Regulatory Ambiguity**: Prominence requirements (e.g., UK Media Act 2024) lack clear definitions, making enforcement difficult

**Evidence from Markets:**
- **Germany**: ARD/ZDF published position paper on HbbTV requirements (2024) - concern about content access
- **Belgium**: VRT Max pre-installation mandate - regulatory response to prominence concerns
- **Joyn Legal Issue (2025)**: Attempted to integrate ARD/ZDF Mediathek without permission - highlights content discovery challenges

**Impact on Broadcasters:**
- Reduced audience reach
- Lower engagement with local content
- Difficulty competing with global platforms
- Regulatory compliance challenges

### 1.2 Distribution Costs & Platform Fragmentation

**The Problem:**
- **Multiple Platform Porting**: Broadcasters must port apps to Samsung Tizen, LG webOS, Android TV, Fire TV, Apple TV, Roku
- **Platform-Specific Requirements**: Each platform has different certification, testing, and whitelisting requirements
- **Ongoing Maintenance**: Multiple codebases, platform-specific updates, compatibility testing
- **Technical Complexity**: Different formats, DRM requirements, API integrations per platform
- **Resource Intensity**: Significant engineering resources required for multi-platform support

**Evidence from Markets:**
- **Germany**: ARD Mediathek, ZDFmediathek, RTL+, Joyn all require porting to multiple platforms
- **OTT Analysis**: All major OTT players require porting to Tizen, webOS, Android TV, Fire TV, Apple TV
- **Certification Costs**: Samsung Tizen, LG webOS require certification and compliance testing
- **Platform Fragmentation**: No single platform dominates, requiring support for all

**Impact on Broadcasters:**
- High development and maintenance costs
- Slower time-to-market for new features
- Resource allocation away from content production
- Technical debt accumulation

### 1.3 Audience Reach & Growth

**The Problem:**
- **Aging Demographics**: Traditional TV audiences aging (only 20% of adults under 30 watch local TV news)
- **Platform Migration**: Viewers shifting from traditional platforms (satellite/cable declining) to internet-based consumption
- **Fragmented Viewing**: Audiences spread across multiple platforms and devices
- **Discovery Challenges**: Difficult to reach new audiences on smart TV platforms
- **Competition**: Competing with global streaming services for attention

**Evidence from Markets:**
- **Germany**: Cable declining -11.8%, Satellite declining -3.1%, Internet 43% primarily use internet for TV
- **Denmark**: 65% of video consumption via streaming, traditional TV viewing declined 10+ minutes daily
- **Switzerland**: 61% internet-based TV, DTT discontinued 2019
- **Czech Republic**: IPTV/cable 46% (overtook DTT) - interactive services preferred

**Impact on Broadcasters:**
- Declining linear TV audiences
- Need to reach younger demographics
- Platform migration challenges
- Audience fragmentation

### 1.4 Data & Analytics

**The Problem:**
- **Limited Audience Insights**: Difficulty understanding viewer behavior, preferences, engagement
- **Fragmented Data**: Data spread across multiple platforms, difficult to aggregate
- **Lack of Cross-Platform Analytics**: Cannot track user journey across devices
- **Insufficient Targeting Data**: Limited data for addressable TV advertising
- **Competitive Disadvantage**: Global platforms have superior data capabilities

**Evidence from Markets:**
- **Addressable TV Growth**: 43% of large advertisers planning to increase addressable TV spending in 2026
- **Data Integration**: Partnerships like Bitmovin-ThinkAnalytics, Nielsen-Amazon Ads show importance of data
- **Targeting Needs**: Broadcasters need better audience segmentation for advertising

**Impact on Broadcasters:**
- Reduced advertising revenue potential
- Limited ability to personalize content
- Difficulty optimizing content strategy
- Competitive disadvantage vs. data-rich platforms

### 1.5 Revenue Generation & Monetization

**The Problem:**
- **Advertising Revenue Pressure**: Streaming revenue surpassed public TV revenue in Europe (2024)
- **Addressable TV Opportunity**: 63% of advertisers who haven't used addressable TV plan to start in 2026
- **Platform Revenue Share**: App stores and platforms take revenue share
- **Monetization Complexity**: Balancing free-to-air mission with revenue needs
- **Competition**: Competing with ad-supported global platforms

**Evidence from Markets:**
- **Germany**: RTL+ 5.6M paying subscribers, Joyn 12.4M monthly users - revenue models vary
- **France**: TF1+ 78% awareness, 33M monthly users - free platform with advertising
- **Addressable TV**: ATVI initiative (RTL Deutschland & ProSiebenSat.1) - addressable TV partnerships

**Impact on Broadcasters:**
- Revenue pressure from streaming competition
- Need for new revenue streams
- Addressable TV opportunity
- Platform revenue share costs

### 1.6 Regulatory Compliance & Must-Carry

**The Problem:**
- **Must-Carry Rules**: Public service broadcasters must be carried on all platforms
- **Prominence Requirements**: Regulatory requirements for content prominence (e.g., Belgium VRT Max mandate)
- **Compliance Complexity**: Different regulations per country, evolving requirements
- **Enforcement Challenges**: Difficult to ensure compliance across multiple platforms
- **Regulatory Ambiguity**: Unclear definitions of "appropriate prominence"

**Evidence from Markets:**
- **Belgium**: VRT Max pre-installation mandate (Flemish Government) - regulatory response
- **Germany**: Must-carry rules for ARD/ZDF on all platforms
- **ARD/ZDF Position Paper**: Published HbbTV requirements (2024) - regulatory engagement

**Impact on Broadcasters:**
- Compliance costs
- Regulatory risk
- Need for platform partnerships
- Prominence enforcement challenges

### 1.7 Technical Infrastructure & Innovation

**The Problem:**
- **HbbTV vs Proprietary Apps**: Need to support both HbbTV (broadcast-triggered) and proprietary apps
- **Infrastructure Costs**: CDN, streaming infrastructure, DRM, analytics
- **Technology Evolution**: Need to adapt to new standards (DVB-I, HbbTV 2.0, 5G Broadcast)
- **Innovation Pressure**: Need to compete with global platforms on technology
- **Resource Constraints**: Limited resources for technical innovation

**Evidence from Markets:**
- **HbbTV Support**: Public service broadcasters heavily use HbbTV (ARD, ZDF, ORF, SRG SSR)
- **DVB-I Development**: German DVB-I pilot, ARD/ZDF leadership
- **5G Broadcast**: NPO active in 5G Broadcast trials
- **Technology Partnerships**: Verance watermarking, ATVI addressable TV

**Impact on Broadcasters:**
- High infrastructure costs
- Technology evolution pressure
- Innovation resource constraints
- Standards compliance requirements
