# TV Platform Operator: Norlys

## 1. Executive Summary

- **Overview of the operator's business**: Norlys is Denmark's largest integrated energy and telecommunications group, offering cable TV, broadband internet, mobile, and energy services. The company operates primarily in Denmark's cable and fiber markets.

- **Key market position**: Largest integrated energy and telecommunications group in Denmark, ~1.5 million customers (post-Telia acquisition, 2024)

- **Geographic footprint**: Nationwide coverage in Denmark

- **Ownership structure**: Independent Danish company

- **Overall growth/decline assessment**: Growth - Strategic acquisition of Telia Denmark (April 2024) significantly expanded customer base and market position. Integration costs impacted 2024 results, but positions company for future growth.

## 4. Growth Assessment

### 4.1 Market Position

- Ranking vs. competitors: Largest integrated energy and telecommunications group in Denmark
- Strength in broadband, mobile, and TV: Strong in cable TV, broadband, mobile (post-Telia), energy services
- Differentiators: Integrated energy and telecom services, fiber infrastructure focus, Telia acquisition provides scale

### 4.2 Growth Indicators

- Subscriber trends across FTTH, broadband, TV, and mobile: Significant growth through Telia acquisition (~1.5 million customers added)
- ARPU trends: Data not publicly disclosed
- Network upgrades: Fiber infrastructure expansion, mobile network integration
- New service launches: Mobile services (post-Telia), enhanced broadband services, integrated offerings

### 4.3 Decline Indicators

- Cord-cutting pressure: Moderate pressure from OTT competitors
- Regulatory or pricing pressure: Standard regulatory environment
- Content rights losses: Standard content portfolio maintained
- Competitive erosion from OTT: Competition from Netflix, Disney+, Amazon Prime Video, but also offers integrated services

### **Overall Assessment**

- **Operator Status:** Growth

- **Drivers Behind the Trend:** Strategic acquisition of Telia Denmark (April 2024, DKK 6.25 billion enterprise value) significantly expanded customer base to ~1.5 million, including mobile network assets (31 retail stores, 4,300 mobile masts). Integration costs exceeded DKK 400 million in 2024, resulting in DKK 60 million loss after tax, but positions company for future growth. Fiber infrastructure focus and integrated energy-telecom model provide competitive advantages. Mobile network acquisition enables multi-play offerings and market expansion.
