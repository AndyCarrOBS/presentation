# TV Platform Operator: PYUR (Tele Columbus)

## 1. Executive Summary

- **Overview of the operator's business**: PYUR (operated by Tele Columbus AG) is a regional cable operator in Germany, providing TV, broadband internet, and telephony services. The company focuses on regional markets, particularly in eastern and central Germany, offering cable-based services to residential and business customers.

- **Key market position**: Regional cable operator, ~5% pay-TV market share, ~1-2 million TV subscribers, ~2.9 million connected residential units (2024)

- **Geographic footprint**: Regional coverage primarily in eastern and central Germany, with focus on secondary markets and smaller cities

- **Ownership structure**: Tele Columbus AG (German-listed company), operating under the PYUR brand

- **Overall growth/decline assessment**: Challenged - Revenue declined 5.7% in 2024, TV subscriber base impacted by regulatory changes, but showing growth in internet and telephony services. ARPU increased significantly (+19.3%) driven by internet services and hardware sales.

## 4. Growth Assessment

### 4.1 Market Position

- Ranking vs. competitors: Regional cable operator, ~5% pay-TV market share
- Strength in broadband, mobile, and TV: Strong in regional cable markets, growing internet services, no mobile presence
- Differentiators: Regional focus, competitive pricing, triple-play bundles, fiber network investments

### 4.2 Growth Indicators

- Subscriber trends across FTTH, broadband, TV, and mobile: Internet services showing growth, TV declining due to regulatory changes, fiber investments ongoing
- ARPU trends: Significant ARPU growth (+19.3%) driven by internet services, hardware sales, and other income streams
- Network upgrades: High investments in fiber optic networks, gigabit network expansion
- New service launches: Enhanced internet services, fiber expansion, service improvements

### 4.3 Decline Indicators

- Cord-cutting pressure: Moderate pressure from OTT competitors
- Regulatory or pricing pressure: Significant impact from regulatory changes affecting cable TV fees (2024), termination of externally supplied portfolios
- Content rights losses: Standard content portfolio maintained
- Competitive erosion from OTT: Competition from Netflix, Disney+, Amazon Prime Video, but also offers integrated OTT services

### **Overall Assessment**

- **Operator Status:** Challenged / Transition

- **Drivers Behind the Trend:** Regulatory changes in 2024 significantly impacted TV revenue and subscriber base, causing 5.7% revenue decline and loss of ~636,000 RGUs (primarily TV). However, company is transitioning focus to internet and telephony services, which are showing growth. ARPU increased significantly (+19.3%) driven by internet services and hardware sales, offsetting TV revenue declines. High investments in fiber optic networks position company for future growth. Management expects stable revenues and reduced capital expenditures in 2025, with increased EBITDA due to lower one-off expenses.
